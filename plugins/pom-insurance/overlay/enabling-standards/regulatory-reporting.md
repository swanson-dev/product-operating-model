# Regulatory reporting — insurance starter standard

> Customise to your carrier's actual regulatory footprint. This is a starter.

## Scope

Every state DOI filing, federal regulatory report, NAIC submission, and contractual regulator commitment the portfolio is responsible for. Includes rate filings, form filings, market-conduct disclosures, AML/BSA reports (if applicable), data-call responses.

Out of scope: tax filings (your finance team's concern); HR/EEO reports (people-ops).

## Baseline rules

### R1 — Filing watchlist exists and is current
A single watchlist (`enabling/regulatory-reporting/watchlist.md` or equivalent) lists every active regulatory commitment with: type, jurisdiction, deadline, owner. Reviewed at least quarterly.

### R2 — Each filing has a named owner
Every filing on the watchlist has a single accountable owner (a person, not a team). The owner approves changes in scope or method.

### R3 — Filing-affecting changes route through compliance
Any product change that affects a filing (rate, form, advertising, agent commissions, broker compensation) requires compliance sign-off recorded in the Discovery shaping log BEFORE Q4 can be marked ✅.

### R4 — SERFF (or equivalent) tracking
For state filings, SERFF tracking numbers are recorded in the product's `runway/` notes when the filing is submitted.

### R5 — Materiality threshold for board / executive escalation
Defined materiality threshold for escalating filing risk to the board or executive committee. Threshold is in `enabling/regulatory-reporting/escalation-policy.md` (starter scaffold).

## Open questions for the standard owner

- What is the cadence for watchlist review — quarterly is the baseline; some portfolios go monthly.
- Where does the SERFF user roster live (procurement / IT-managed)?
- Who is the named compliance partner for the Discovery sign-off (R3)?
- What is the dollar / policyholder threshold for board escalation (R5)?

## How this interacts with Q4

Insurance-specific Q4 sub-question I1 (regulatory exposure) reads this standard. A DISC affecting a filing without R3 sign-off cannot be marked I1 ✅.
