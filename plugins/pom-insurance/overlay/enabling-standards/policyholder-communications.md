# Policyholder communications — insurance starter standard

> Customise to your carrier's brand voice and applicable state plain-language regulations.

## Scope

Every customer-facing written communication that affects a policyholder's understanding of their coverage, their claim, their rights, or their account. Includes: policy documents, claim acknowledgements / denials / payments, lapse notices, renewal notices, rate-change notices, cancellation notices, beneficiary correspondence, regulatory disclosures, EOBs (health).

Out of scope: marketing copy (your brand team's concern); agent-facing internal communications.

## Baseline rules

### P1 — Plain language bar
Communications are written at or below a defined reading-grade level (Flesch-Kincaid 8 or lower for consumer-facing) unless a regulator-required disclosure forces specific verbatim language. Where regulator language is required, plain-language explanation accompanies it.

### P2 — Vulnerable-context handling
Communications affecting policyholders in vulnerable moments (denial, large claim, lapse for non-payment, beneficiary correspondence after a death claim) get an extra editorial review for tone, clarity, and escalation paths.

### P3 — Accessibility
All communications meet the generic accessibility-baseline (X1–X7 from pom-core). For multilingual markets, communications are available in any language the portfolio is licensed to write in.

### P4 — Escalation/appeals path surfaced
Every adverse communication (denial, rate increase, cancellation) names the appeals or escalation path: who to contact, how, what information to include.

### P5 — Versioning and effective date
Each communication template has a version and effective date; superseded versions are archived (not deleted) for compliance reconstruction.

### P6 — State-of-issue compliance
Where state regulations dictate specific content, font sizes, or notice timelines, the relevant requirements are tracked per state and the communication renders correctly per state of issue.

## Open questions for the standard owner

- What is the editorial review queue for P2-flagged communications? Who owns it?
- For multilingual markets, what is the QA process for translated copy?
- How are state-specific variants of a single communication maintained — one master with variation logic, or one template per state?

## How this interacts with Q4

Insurance-specific Q4 sub-questions I3 (policyholder vulnerable populations) and I7 (claim-experience transparency) read this standard. Generic G1 (vulnerable users) and G3 (consent & transparency) also apply.
