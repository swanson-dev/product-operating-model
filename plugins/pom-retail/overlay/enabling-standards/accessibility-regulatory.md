# Accessibility — regulatory overlay (retail)

> Companion to `pom-core`'s generic `accessibility-baseline.md` (X1–X7). The generic baseline defines the WCAG floor; this overlay adds the regulatory enforcement layer for retail surfaces.

## Scope

Every customer-facing surface in the retail context: e-commerce site, mobile app, retail kiosks, self-checkout, gift cards, receipts (paper and digital), marketing emails, store-locator, store-pickup confirmation flows. ADA Title III applies to US e-commerce by enforcement precedent; EAA applies to EU markets effective 2025; specific state laws layer on top.

## Baseline rules (in addition to generic X1–X7)

### AR1 — Surface enumeration
The portfolio maintains an enumeration of customer-facing surfaces with their accessibility-conformance status. Updated at least quarterly and after any material surface addition.

### AR2 — VPAT (Voluntary Product Accessibility Template) currency
For procured technology (e.g., POS systems, payment terminals, kiosk software), vendor VPATs are on file and reviewed against actual conformance — vendor self-attestation is not sufficient evidence on its own.

### AR3 — Real-user testing with assistive tech
At minimum annually, real users using screen readers / switch controls / magnification / voice control test the most-used journeys (PDP → cart → checkout → confirmation; account creation; return initiation; store-locator). Findings drive remediation.

### AR4 — Receipts and confirmations
Paper receipts have minimum font sizes; digital receipts/confirmations meet WCAG; gift cards have braille or accessible alternative where required by state.

### AR5 — Conformance gap remediation backlog
ADA/EAA conformance gaps are tracked as PB items with owners and target dates. Aged gaps escalate to executive review.

### AR6 — Demand-letter and complaint response readiness
Documented process for responding to accessibility demand letters / DOJ complaints / state AG actions. Legal partner identified in advance.

### AR7 — Procurement gate
New procurement (third-party customer-facing tech) includes accessibility review as a procurement gate; non-conformant vendors require recorded exception.
